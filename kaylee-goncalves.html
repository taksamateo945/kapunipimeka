What happened to Kaylee Goncalves? From Dreams of Journalism to Tragic End

July 2024 · 4 minute read

Kaylee Goncalves was a 21-year-old student at the University of Idaho who was tragically killed along with three other students.

Her death has left her family and friends devastated and has sparked outrage across the country.

In this article, we will take a closer look at Kaylee’s life and the events that led up to her untimely death.

Background

Kaylee Goncalves was born on January 1st, 2002 in Boise, Idaho.

She grew up in a small town called Meridian and attended Meridian High School.

After graduating from high school, Kaylee decided to attend the University of Idaho to pursue a degree in journalism.

She was known for her outgoing personality and her love for writing.

In her free time, Kaylee enjoyed hiking and spending time with her friends and family.

What happened to Kaylee Goncalves?

On the night of May 20th, 2023, Kaylee Goncalves and three other students were killed in a shooting at a house party in Moscow, Idaho.

According to police reports, the shooter was Bryan Kohberger who was a former student at the University of Idaho. Kohberger was armed with a handgun and opened fire on the students, killing Kaylee and three others.

The shooter was later apprehended by police and is currently awaiting trial.

The suspect: Bryan Kohberger

After the shooting, police launched an investigation into the incident.

They interviewed witnesses and collected evidence from the scene of the crime.

According to police reports, Kohberger had a history of mental illness and had been expelled from the University of Idaho for threatening behavior.

The investigation also revealed that Kohberger had purchased the handgun used in the shooting legally from a local gun store.

The Bryan Kohberger case

Bryan Kohberger appeared for his arraignment in a Latah County Court on May 23rd, 2023 after he was indicted on four counts of first-degree murder and one count of burglary for the killings of Kaylee Goncalves, Madison Mogen, Xana Kernodle, and Ethan Chapin.

During the arraignment, Kohberger stood silent as a judge entered five not guilty pleas for him.

Kaylee Goncalves’s father said he felt “rage” in the courtroom as he attended Bryan Kohberger’s arraignment.

The families of two of the University of Idaho murder victims have indicated that they plan to file a motion calling for the death penalty for Bryan Kohberger.
