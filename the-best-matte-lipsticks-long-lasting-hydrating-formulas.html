With its velvety texture and rich pigmentation, matte lipstick has the power to add instant glamour to any look. It also happens to last longer than the average lip tint or satin-finish lipstick, making it a must for big events and days when touch-ups aren’t an option. It’s no wonder that makeup artists turn to their trusted matte lipsticks for important occasions, like the red carpet and TV appearances, weddings, and other major moments when clients need to put their best face forward (without actually thinking about their face).

“The magic of matte lipsticks versus lipstick with a satin or high-shine finish is that they are super long-lasting and have the ultimate staying power compared to their glossy or satin finish counterparts,” says Olivia Todd, a UK-based makeup artist specializing in editorial and luxury bridal looks. “If my client is attending an all-day event, I typically opt for a matte lip,” celebrity makeup artist Jamie Greenberg agrees. “I trust that the matte formula will stay put and last all day without having to constantly reapply.” Because matte lipstick formulas are designed to set and dry in place, they do not smudge once applied. Taylor Swift’s iconic red lipstick is a great example of this phenomenon—even after performing a three-hour show, you can always trust her matte red lips to look perfectly fresh. Wondering which ones are her favorites? Keep reading, we have at least two on this list.

Whether you’re just dipping a toe into the wonderful world of matte lipstick or you’re looking to expand your collection with a bold new shade, look no further—we tracked down the best matte lipstick formulas of 2024 and got the scoop on each formula’s pros and cons from Todd and Greenberg. From hydrating formulas made to quench even the driest lips to designer options that deliver rich color with luxurious appeal, we’re obsessed with these matte lipsticks—and you will be, too.

What to look for in a mate lipstick

We love matte lipsticks for their rich color and velvety texture, but the main reason we reach for them time and again is longevity. But because they’re made to be smudge-proof, certain matte lipsticks can feel dry on the skin or emphasize chapped areas. If your lips tend to get dry and chapped easily, opt for hydrating matte lipsticks such as MAC Powder Kiss Velvet Blur Slim Moisturizing Matte Lipstick ($28) or Pat McGrath Labs LiquiLUST Legendary Wear Matte Lipstick ($34). “Those with dry skin should prep the lips beforehand with a hydrating lip balm or mask,” Todd advises. “If you have oilier skin, matte lipstick is a great option as it balances oil and will last all day.” She also notes that occasion plays an important role, so be sure to think about where and when you’ll be wearing matte lipstick. “A sophisticated evening occasion may call for a matte lipstick to add drama, whereas, for a fun daytime activity, a more natural or glossy look may be better suited.”

What are the best shades for matte lipstick?

Matte lipstick comes in a wide range of shades that look beautiful on every skin tone, so choosing the best one for you will come down to the look you’re trying to create. “A matte red is of course very chic and classic, but you can also create a very natural look using mauves, nudes, and pinks,” says Greenberg. “Just remember that matte lipstick can be as bold or as natural as you want it to be. Have fun with it!”

I personally own several matte lipsticks ranging from classic nudes to romantic pinks and coral, as well as a handful of vibrant pinks and deep reds. While I typically reach for the more vibrant colors when I’m heading out to a special event and want to make a bold statement, more often than not, I wear nude lipsticks from day to night. I love their versatility and the sense of polish they bring to my everyday makeup look—plus, I find that nude shades draw more attention to my eyes.

Is matte lipstick ideal for mature skin?

Good news for those with mature skin or particularly dry lips: matte lipstick can absolutely work for you. Just be sure to choose a lipstick that won’t settle into lines, and ensure that you’ve fully prepped your lips before applying it. “It’s important to take the proper steps to prevent any feathering or bleeding, as mature skin tends to have more fine lines surrounding the lip area,” Greenberg explains. “As long as you prep your skin and lips, anyone at any age can rock a matte lip.” She recommends Chanel Rouge Allure Velvet Matte Lipstick ($48) for mature skin, as it’s a super-hydrating formula that comes in a gorgeous selection of shades.
